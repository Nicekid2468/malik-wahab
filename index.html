About

Systems Thinking,
Applied Everywhere

I am a Mechanical Engineering graduate of the University of Ilorin — a discipline that taught me something far broader than thermodynamics and material science: it taught me how to see any environment as a system. From stress distribution in a steel truss to supply-chain velocity in a high-volume depot, the engineering lens remains the same — identify inputs, optimise processes, and deliver measurable outputs.

That systems-thinking now powers a dual career. On the operations side, I have managed logistics and quality-control workflows for fast-moving consumer goods, driving efficiency across physical depot operations. On the digital side, I architect brand identities, automate marketing pipelines, and direct cinematic AI-generated content — applying the same rigour and precision I learned in the engineering lab.

The result is a rare hybrid: a professional who can translate between warehouse floor and creative studio, between Excel models and AI image-generation tools, between procurement contracts and brand positioning strategy.
